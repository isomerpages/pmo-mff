<h4><strong>Government-Paid Paternity Leave</strong></h4>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="Government-Paid Paternity Leave" src="/images/Government_Paid_Maternity_Leave.png">
</div>
<p>Government-Paid Paternity Leave <strong>provides working fathers</strong> with
time off from work to care for their newborn and wife as she recovers from
childbirth.</p>
<p><strong>For children born before 1 January 2024:</strong> Eligible working
fathers are entitled to <strong>two weeks</strong> of Government-Paid Paternity
Leave.</p>
<p><strong>For children born or adopted on or after 1 January 2024<sup>1,2</sup>:</strong> Eligible
working fathers may take an additional two weeks of Government-Paid Paternity
Leave i.e. <strong>four weeks in total</strong>. The additional two weeks
are given on a voluntary basis and subjected to the employer's agreement.
Employers who are ready to grant the additional two weeks of leave will
be reimbursed by the Government.</p>
<p>By default, Government-Paid Paternity Leave should be taken in a <strong>two- or four-week block within 16 weeks from your child's date of birth</strong>,
If there is a mutual agreement between you and your employer, you can take
the paternity leave flexibly, but should do so within 12 months from your
child’s date of birth.</p>
<p>You are eligible for <strong>Government-Paid Paternity Leave</strong> if
you meet all the following criteria:
<br>
<br>1. Your child is a Singapore Citizen at birth, or in the case of a stillborn
child, would have been a Singapore Citizen at birth.
<br>2. You are lawfully married to the child's mother sometime between conception
and birth.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>If not, you can be eligible for the Government-Paid Paternity Leave if
you are legally married to your child’s mother within 12 months from your
child’s date of birth.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><em>Note: The Government-Paid Paternity Leave can only be taken after you are married and within 12 months from your child’s date of birth.</em>
</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>3. a. Employees: You have worked for your employer for a continuous period
of at least three months before your child is born.
<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; b. Self-employed: You have been engaged in a particular
business, trade, profession or vocation for a continuous period of at least
three months before your child is born, and have lost income because you
did not engage in your business, trade or profession during the leave period.</p>
<p>(Note 1: Fathers of children with an Estimated Date of Delivery (EDD)
that is on or after 1 January 2024 will also be eligible for the additional
two weeks of GPPL. Eligible fathers will need to email a scanned copy of
your doctor's certification on your child's EDD to <a href="https://www.madeforfamilies.gov.sg/parental-leave-and-benefits/government-paid-paternity-leave#" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">contactus@profamilyleave.gov.sg</a>.
The doctor's certification should include the following details:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>EDD;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Mother's name and identification number;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Name of clinic; and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Doctor's signature/Medical Registraction (MCR) number.)</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>(Note 2: The additional two weeks of GPPL do not apply to fathers of a
stillborn child.)</p>
<p>If you are an <strong>adoptive father</strong>, you are eligible for <strong>Government-Paid Paternity Leave</strong> if
you meet all the following criteria:</p>
<p>1. The child you are adopting is a Singapore Citizen and you have made
an application to the Court to adopt him or her.</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>If your child is not a Singapore Citizen, a Dependant’s Pass has been
issued by the Ministry of Social and Family Development in respect of the
child; and either you or your spouse (if she is a joint applicant to the
adoption) has been a Singapore Citizen since the date when the pass was
issued.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>2. a. Employees: You have worked for your employer for at least three
continuous months preceding the Formal Intent to Adopt date*.</p>
<p>&nbsp; &nbsp; b. Self-employed: You have been engaged in a particular
business, trade, profession or vocation for a continuous period of at least
three continuous months preceding the Formal Intent to Adopt date, and
have lost income as a result of not engaging in your business, trade or
profession during the leave period.</p>
<p>3. You are not the biological father of the child.</p>
<p>If you are an <strong>adoptive father</strong>, the Government/employer
may recover the whole or any part of the payment for <strong>Government-Paid Paternity Leave </strong>reimbursed/granted
if:</p>
<p>a. Adoption Order for the child is not granted within 12 months from the
Formal Intent to Adopt (FIA) date (inclusive of FIA date); or
<br>b. Your child is not a Singapore Citizen by birth and does not become
one within six months from the Adoption Order date (inclusive of Adoption
Order date).</p>
<p>*Formal Intent to Adopt:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>For a Singapore Citizen child: the date on which an application to adopt
is made to the Court; or</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>For a child who is not a Singapore Citizen: the date on which a Dependant’s
Pass was issued by the Ministry of Social and Family Development in respect
of the child, or a document indicating the application for the pass has
been approved.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Paternity Leave is paid by the Government. Reimbursement is capped at
$2,500 per week i.e., total of up to $10,000 for four weeks, depending
on the duration of leave taken. The caps are inclusive of CPF contributions.</p>
<p>Visit the <a href="https://www.profamilyleave.msf.gov.sg/schemes/paternity-leave" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>Pro-Family Leave website</u></a>&nbsp;for
more details on the application process.</p>
